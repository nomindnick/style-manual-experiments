## Flags for the human

No hallucinated citations detected in the seed list. Two small caption-style notes (non-blocking):

- **Carma Developers**: CourtListener's caption spells out "(California)" where the seed list abbreviates "(Cal.)". Both forms appear in California practice; the seed list's form is fine for court filings.
- **Katsura v. City of San Buenaventura**: CourtListener renders the plaintiff surname in ALL CAPS ("KATSURA"). That's a CL-side display quirk, not a citation problem.

Two subdivision-shape notes that drafting subagents should honor (to avoid inventing structure that doesn't exist):

- **Ed. Code §§ 17604, 17605** and **Gov. Code § 53060** have **no lettered subdivisions**. A cite like "Ed. Code, § 17604, subd. (a)" would be hallucinated.
- **Civ. Code §§ 1550 and 1565** use **numbered clauses** ("1., 2., 3.") rather than lettered subdivisions. A cite like "Civ. Code, § 1550, subd. (a)" would be hallucinated; the correct form is "Civ. Code, § 1550" (optionally narrowing to clause 3, etc., but not using subd. (a)).
